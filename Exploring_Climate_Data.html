Climate Data: Earth surface temperature 1900 to 2012¶

Dataset pulled from Kaggle.

This set of average monthly temperature data from Berkley goes as far back as 1743 for some countries. For this analysis, let's take a look at how things have changed post-industrialization. Climate science tells us each degree matters as far as impact to the planet so we are curious to find a baseline temperature and see how the yearly average for each country departs from our baseline.

First, we need to load the data into pandas as a dataframe.

More Filtering¶

With all the incomplete temp readings and missing years of info, comparing each country won't be as easy. Maybe limiting our search to the top 10 countries in size would be another starting point. According to World Atlas, the top 10 largest countries make up 49% of the earth's landmass. They collectively span 5 different continents offering "every type of climate imaginable."

Using the countries from the World Atlas list, I'll filter our dataframe again...

In [11]:
top10 = ['Algeria', 'Kazakhstan', 'Argentina', 'India', 'Australia', 'Brazil', 'China', 'United States', 'Canada', 'Russia']

years= range(1900,2014) 
for x in years:
    for y in top10: 
        check= df[(df.Year==x) & (df.Country==y)].Year.value_counts() #Filter the dataframe to each Year and Country in our range and list. value_counts() will give us the number of occurences

Averaging temperatures¶

Next we want to get the average yearly temperature by Country and be able to compare it to a baseline average from all the readings from 1900-2012. This will tell us if our yearly reading is above or below average and better determine if there is a general trend. Average yearly temp and temp uncertainty will be calculated as well.

In [17]:
df["AverageYearlyTemp"] = df.groupby(["Country", "Year"])['AverageTemperature'].transform('mean')
df["AverageYearlyTempUncertainty"] = df.groupby(["Country", "Year"])['AverageTemperatureUncertainty'].transform('mean')
df["Top10AverageTemp"] = df.groupby(["Country"])["AverageYearlyTemp"].transform('mean') #Use this for baseline
